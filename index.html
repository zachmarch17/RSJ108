How to Plant Dahlias Like a Journalist

How to Plant Dahlias Like a Journalist

By Zach March
5 simple steps for beautiful blooms

Crafting the perfect dahlia garden is a lot like writing a compelling article: it requires patience, attention to detail, and the right tools. This guide will walk you through each step to ensure your dahlias bloom with the confidence and color of a front-page story.

  • Things You'll Need

  • A dahlia tuber
  • Fertile soil
  • Irrigation system
  • Watering pail
  • Gopher repellent
  • A shovel

  1. Prepare Your Foundation

    Before you begin planting, ensure your irrigation system is properly set up. Dahlias thrive in fertile, well-drained soil, so it’s essential to have that in place before introducing your tuber.

    Preparing the soil and irrigation system is key to a healthy dahlia garden.

  2. Dig the Perfect Hole

    Using a shovel, dig a hole approximately 4 to 6 inches deep. When placing the dahlia tuber in the ground, position the “eye” of the tuber at a slight incline. This orientation supports healthy sprouting and long-term growth.

    Digging the hole to ensure proper depth for the dahlia tuber.

  3. Firm the Soil Around the Tuber

    Once the tuber is in place, pack it in by gently adding soil around it. Use your hands to press and mold the dirt, ensuring the tuber is snug and supported. This step provides a strong foundation for the plant to grow.

    Firming the soil around the tuber to create a stable base.

  4. Guard Against Gophers

    Mix gopher repellent into the remaining soil that was dug out. Thoroughly combine the repellent with the soil before using it to cover the tuber. This added protection helps safeguard your plant from underground pests.

    Adding gopher repellent to protect the tuber from pests.

  5. Water Wisely

    Finish by watering the newly planted tuber. Be cautious with the amount of water — too much moisture early on can cause the tuber to rot. For best results, refer to the recommended watering technique in the accompanying video.

    Watering the planted tuber with care to avoid excess moisture.
